# Pre-Purchase Inspection in Missoula, MT

A pre-purchase inspection is a mechanical evaluation of a used vehicle conducted by an independent technician before you buy. In Missoula, where used vehicles regularly come off high-mileage rural commutes, seasonal road conditions, and out-of-state sales, the condition described by a seller often misses what's actually happening underneath. We put the car on a lift, run a full diagnostic scan, and give you a documented account of what the vehicle needs now, what's likely coming, and whether the asking price reflects any of it.

---

## What This Service Covers

- **Engine and fluid check** — oil condition and level, coolant, transmission fluid, power steering fluid, and active leaks at seals, gaskets, and hose fittings
- **OBD-II diagnostic scan** — reads stored and active trouble codes across engine, transmission, ABS, airbag, and emissions systems
- **Brake system evaluation** — pad thickness, rotor condition and measurement, caliper function, brake fluid condition and moisture level
- **Suspension and steering inspection** — ball joints, tie rod ends, CV axles and boots, control arm bushings, shocks and struts for leaks and play
- **Underbody and frame inspection** — rust penetration depth, prior repair welds, impact damage, and subframe condition
- **Transmission check** — fluid color and smell, external leaks, shift quality during test drive
- **Tire condition** — tread depth at multiple points, uneven wear patterns, sidewall damage
- **Electrical systems** — battery health under load, charging system output, lighting function, power accessories
- **HVAC function** — vent temperature output for heat and A/C, fan speeds, cabin air filter condition
- **Test drive** — handling, braking response, noise under load, shift behavior, and steering pull

## Common Symptoms

A pre-purchase inspection is appropriate for any used vehicle purchase. These situations make it especially important:

- Check engine light present or recently cleared before listing
- High mileage with no service records provided
- Price significantly below market for the make, model, and year
- Out-of-state vehicle with unknown road salt exposure or climate history
- Seller claims "just serviced" with no documentation
- Previous accident disclosed on the vehicle history report
- Musty, oily, or burned smell in the cabin or near the engine bay
- Visible rust on body panels, rocker panels, or wheel arches
- Vehicle pulls to one side during a test drive
- Fluid stains under the car where it's been sitting

## Why It Happens

Most used car problems aren't obvious at a glance or during a short test drive. A worn front wheel bearing, for example, may only produce noise above 55 mph — easily missed during a neighborhood loop. A failing automatic transmission can shift acceptably at light throttle and slip noticeably under load. Head gasket degradation doesn't always show in oil color or visible coolant loss early on; it shows up in a coolant pressure test or in combustion gas testing at the radiator neck.

Deferred maintenance compounds quietly. A timing chain that's stretched past spec, untreated oil weeping at valve cover or rear main seals, corroded battery terminals that test marginal under load — none of this registers in a walk-around. The [battery and charging system](/services/battery-charging-system) in particular tends to be overlooked by sellers and buyers alike until a vehicle fails to start at –10°F.

Frame damage from prior accidents is the hardest category to catch without a lift. Collision repair quality varies, and a structurally compromised vehicle can be repainted and detailed to show clean. Uneven panel gaps, inconsistent paint depth between adjacent panels, and unusual undercoating patterns around frame rails are what an experienced eye looks for — none of it visible from a standard test drive or a vehicle history report alone. For more detail on what a thorough inspection involves, see the [used car inspection guide](/services/used-car-inspection-guide).

## How We Diagnose It

### Inspect
We start on a lift. Full underbody view — frame rails, floor pans, subframe mounting points, suspension components, CV axle boots, brake lines, and exhaust. We're checking for rust depth (surface vs. penetrating), prior weld repairs, and impact damage. Fluid leaks are traced to their source: oil pan gasket, rear main seal, axle seals, transmission pan, power steering lines, or radiator connections. Under the hood, we evaluate the serpentine belt, coolant hose condition, battery terminals, and visible valve cover areas for oil tracking.

### Test
We run an OBD-II scan across all available modules — not just the powertrain. ABS codes, airbag system codes, and transmission codes are all logged. A scan that shows recently cleared codes with no freeze frame data is itself informative: it indicates the light was cleared before the sale without addressing the underlying fault. We also load-test the battery and measure alternator output voltage under accessory load.

### Confirm
We cross-reference what we find on the lift and in the scan with a test drive. Suspension noise under compression, brake pedal response under hard stops, transmission behavior at highway speed and under load — these either confirm or rule out what we found statically. Anything inconsistent gets noted specifically.

### Report
You get a written summary organized by urgency: what needs attention before the purchase, what's likely to come up in the next year, and what's in acceptable condition. That breakdown gives you a specific basis to negotiate, ask the seller to address items, or walk away with a clear picture of the risk. ## When To Schedule Service

Schedule a pre-purchase inspection before you sign anything or transfer any money. The best position is to negotiate the inspection as a condition of sale before you've verbally committed to the deal. Most private sellers and dealers won't object to this — and the ones who do are telling you something.

If you're purchasing remotely or from out of state, we can inspect a vehicle held at a local dealer, private lot, or auction facility in the Missoula area. We've also done inspections on behalf of buyers who couldn't travel to see the car in person.

Schedule before your negotiation window closes, not after. Once money changes hands, the information you get from an inspection has no leverage attached to it.

## Local Conditions in Missoula

Missoula's climate introduces wear patterns that affect used vehicles disproportionately. Winters bring road salt and sand application on Highway 93, I-90, and the connector routes into the Bitterroot Valley and Rattlesnake area. Vehicles that have been driven in this environment accumulate salt exposure at brake lines, frame rails, and rocker panels — areas that can look clean from the outside while corroding from within.

The city sits at roughly 3,200 feet, and many daily drives go higher — toward Lolo Pass, the front range, or up into the foothills. The elevation changes and load variations put more demand on cooling systems and transmission fluid than flat-terrain driving does. Temperature swings are also significant: vehicles parked outside through sub-zero Montana winters and driven in summer heat degrade rubber components — hoses, belts, CV boots, and weatherstripping — faster than vehicles from more moderate climates. If the vehicle you're considering has spent its life in western Montana, a lift inspection focused specifically on corrosion is worth the time.
